Overview

NAMLYS aims to foster discussion on emerging numerical and computational challenges in applying machine learning to dynamical systems, with particular emphasis on high-performance computing (HPC), numerical stability, and distributed training.

We welcome contributions exploring the interplay among numerical analysis, machine learning, and HPC in time-dependent systems and evolutionary problems.

How to submit

  1. Read the ICCSA author guidelines on the Author’s page (format, templates, policies).
  2. Prepare your manuscript following the ICCSA instructions (Springer LNCS workflow applies to the proceedings process).
  3. Go to the Electronic Submission System (ESS) and log in / create an account.
  4. In the submission form, select the correct workshop entry: NAMLYS in the list box shown in the Abstract, Paper, and Camera Ready steps.
  5. Upload your paper, verify metadata (title, authors, keywords), and complete the submission.
  6. Keep track of deadlines (see Important dates) and be ready for the camera-ready and registration steps if accepted.
  7. If you have questions, use the Contact organizers option above.

Workshop scope

NAMLYS aims to foster discussion on emerging numerical and computational challenges in applying machine learning to dynamical systems, with particular emphasis on high-performance computing, numerical stability, and distributed training.

Topics of interest (including, but not limited to)

  • HPC-aware parallel and distributed algorithms for deep learning
  • Conditioning and numerical stability in training processes
  • Numerical linear algebra for large-scale learning
  • Finite-precision arithmetic effects
  • Error analysis in backpropagation and automatic differentiation
  • Neural solvers for ODEs and PDEs with adaptive timestepping
  • Accelerator-based implementations (GPUs/TPUs and emerging hardware)
  • Numerical methods in neural PDE solvers
  • Federated learning and decentralized training for physics-based models
